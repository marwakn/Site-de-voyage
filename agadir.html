Crocoparc Agadir

A Favorite Of Nature

          Through a maze of paths and walkways,
          one can observe reptiles in their daily 
          lives: lazing in the sun, bathing and
          demonstrating the power of their jaws 
          during their meal.The circuit, with a 
          length of over 600 meters, is marked 
          by informational signs to learn not
          only about crocodiles, but also about 
          the different plant species which 
          inhabit the crossed gardens.

Taghazout

Surfers Paradise


          For a long time, Taghazout (pronounced
          tarazout) has remained a well-kept 
          secret, a refuge for surfers in search 
          of the perfect wave in Morocco. Since 
          then, the landscape has evolved around
          this small fishing village 40 minutes 
          north of Agadir. On the long bay which
          stretches a little further, a new generation
          seaside resort emerges.

AGADIR,an active destination

Agadir, a Amazigh city located on the southern Atlantic coast of Morocco in the foothills of the Anti-AtlaS. 508 km south of Casablanca, 173 km from Essaouira and 235 km west of Marrakech. With its boulevards lined with palm trees and its bars on the seafront, Agadir, Morocco's 1st seaside resort, has a very Western atmosphere and exceptional climate. Its 10 km long beach, will ensure you an exceptional seaside holiday. The city with 300 days of sunshine per year, is full of activities will make you forget all your worries. .

AGADIR: some argue, more modern

Thousands of years ago, Agadir was a small fishing village. Even today, fishing is still important to the city. Its seafood, incidentally, is excellent. While Agadir may have began as a small fishing village, in the earth sixteenth century, an influx of Portuguese and German settlers began. In 1911, the Agadir Crisis occurred, which resulted in France establishing a protectorate over Morocco. The most traumatic event in recent Agadir history is the earthquake of 1960, in which 15,000 people died. The city was totally destroyed and had to be rebuilt.The new city is now strikingly different from your typical Moroccan city.

AGADIR: One of the jewels of Morocco

Known for its white buildings hidden behind the dunes, its wide flowery boulevards, its modern hotels and its European-style cafes, Agadir is no longer a typical city of traditional Morocco, but it is a modern, active and dynamic city, resolutely looking to the future. Here, the softness is that of fine sand, the caress of the sun.It’s all about laid-back relaxation in Agadir, so ride a camel, rent a beach buggy, check out the Suq al-Had market or take the 20-minute walk up to the ruins of the Agadir Kasbah for expansive city views if you’re taking a break from the beach.
